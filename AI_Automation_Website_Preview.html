Back‑end

How we simplify it behind the scenes

1
One workflow engine (n8n)

Everything runs on one automation brain. Triggers (email, files, webhooks) → processors (OCR, AI, rules) → actions (mail, calendar, Notion, Drive) with retry and logs.

2
Unified payload

We normalize all inputs to a single JSON shape: {source,title,url,text,metadata}. That keeps rules and actions simple.

3
Rules first, AI second

Deterministic rules make results predictable. AI fills the gaps (e.g., extracting entities, tagging, summarizing long content).

4
Least‑privilege connections

Scoped OAuth + BYO keys. Only the permissions required for each action (read‑only when possible).

5
Transparent logs

Every action writes a log row with timestamp, item, rule used, and result so you can audit and roll back.

6
Graceful failure

Retries, dead‑letter queue, and a 5pm summary of anything that needs a quick human nudge.

User Guide

Follow these steps to set it up

1) Connect services (Email, Drive, Notion, Calendar)+
  • Authorize read‑only access for email (Newsletter/Client labels).
  • Pick one Drive/OneDrive folder to watch for PDFs/images.
  • Copy our Notion template bundle (3 databases) into your workspace.
  • Connect Calendar with free/busy scope for smart scheduling.

All connections use least‑privilege scopes; you keep control of your keys.

2) Turn on ingestion & OCR+

We poll email every 10 minutes and watch your chosen Drive folder. PDFs and images get OCR’d to text so the AI can summarize and tag them.

Payload shape: {"{source,title,url,text,metadata}"}
3) Summarize & Tag+

We condense content into 5 bullets and extract entities (people, companies, amounts, dates). The result is saved to Notion “Inbox Items.”

Fields updated: Summary, Entities/Tags, Priority (hint), Suggested Action
4) Prioritize & Decide (Rules + AI)+

Simple JSON rules do most of the work, with AI as a backstop for edge cases.

[
  {"{"}"if":"source=='Email' && text.includes('invoice')","then":{"{"}"priority":"P0","action":"Reply"}},
  {"{"}"if":"entities.includes('flight') && price<250","then":{"{"}"priority":"P0","action":"Notify"}},
  {"{"}"if":"source=='Web' && text.includes('launch')","then":{"{"}"priority":"P1","action":"Draft"}}
]
5) Actions: reply, schedule, draft, file, notify+
  • Reply: AI drafts → send via your mail with you CC’d.
  • Schedule: Propose slots, confirm, and write back to Notion.
  • Draft: Create a Doc/Notion page from template.
  • File: Move/rename files and add cross‑links.
  • Notify: Email/SMS a one‑liner with link to open.
6) Output: Daily Brief, Inbox w/ Action Cards, Automation Logs+

Daily Brief at 9:00 AM: 5 highlights, decisions needed (P0), schedule changes, top links.

Inbox w/ Action Cards: Quick approve, snooze, archive, escalate.

Logs: A full audit trail (who/what/when) that you can export.

Tip Start with Notify only, then add Draft and Schedule after a day of observing the flow.

Troubleshooting

If something looks off

Items not showing up in Inbox+

Check the source connection, then confirm the “watched” label/folder. Manually add a test email or PDF to trigger ingestion.

Summaries feel generic+

Increase the temperature to 0.6 and add a domain hint (“This is about pet‑care operations”).

Rules misfiring+

Review the JSON conditions; test with preview payloads. Keep rules specific and let AI handle the rest.

Too many alerts+

Throttle notifications and push non‑critical updates to the 5pm summary.
